›What makes Wilson good? Is he just a default?
🎩
Wilson
The Gentleman Scientist

Wilson has balanced stats and one perk — growing a magnificent beard. It grows in three stages: Short (day 4), Long (day 8), Magnificent (day 16). The beard provides increasing insulation from freezing, equivalent to warm headgear at full growth.

Shaving yields Beard Hair for crafting Meat Effigies — resurrection items that bring you back on death. This makes Wilson one of the safest characters.

Save the beard for Winter, shave on Day 1 of Spring. The insulation alone delays freezing significantly.
›How does Willow's fire mechanic work?
🔥
Willow
The Firestarter

Willow spawns with a unique Lighter (infinite durability, dimmer than a torch). She is immune to fire damage and regains Sanity near fires (+10/min near large fires).

The danger: when Sanity drops to 60 or below, she randomly ignites fires at her feet. She won't burn — but your base will.

Keep Willow's Sanity above 60 near your base at all times. Retreat to a campfire if it's dropping — fire proximity restores her Sanity.

›How does Wendy summon Abigail?
👻
Wendy
The Bereaved

Wendy spawns with Abigail's Flower. It blooms over 2–4 days. Place it on the ground — when a creature dies near it, Abigail's ghost appears. She deals AoE damage, excelling against swarms of Spiders, Hounds, and Bats.

Wendy hits for 25% less damage than normal, so she depends on Abigail. She also only loses 0.75× Sanity in darkness and near monsters.

Abigail shreds Spider Dens. Lead Wendy to a den at dusk, let Spiders emerge, Abigail handles them, you collect drops.
›How does WX-78 upgrade with Gears?
🤖
WX-78
Soulless Automaton

WX-78 starts at 150/150/150. Each Gear eaten adds roughly +20 HP, +6.67 Hunger, +6.67 Sanity. Max after 15 Gears: 400 HP / 200 Hunger / 300 Sanity.

No penalty from spoiled food. Lightning triggers Overcharge — +50% speed, emits light, +100 HP, lasts a full day. Takes constant damage in rain.

All upgrades lost on death! Gears come from Clockwork enemies, Tumbleweeds, and Graves.

›How does the Werebeaver curse work?
🪓
Woodie
The Lumberjack

Woodie starts with Lucy the Axe — infinite durability, fast chopping, half weapon damage. If he chops too many trees quickly or during a full moon, he transforms into a Werebeaver.

As Werebeaver: stats are replaced by a single Beaverness meter. He gnaws through trees, rocks, and structures rapidly but can't access inventory, map, or crafting. Reverts at low Beaverness, leaving him at 50 HP, 37 Hunger, 50 Sanity.

Key advantage: immune to freezing — a panic button for Winter survival.

›Why would anyone play Wes?
🤡
Wes
The Mime

Wes is the challenge character. Lower HP (113), lower Hunger (113), lower Sanity (150), 0.75× damage, starves 1.25× faster, can't talk. His only ability: blowing Balloons (5 Sanity each) that pop for 5 AoE damage.

Unlocked by finding him trapped in Adventure Mode. Survive 100+ days as Wes = true mastery.
›How do Maxwell's Shadow Puppets work?
🎩
Maxwell
The Puppet Master

Only 75 HP but regenerates Sanity at +20/min passively. Starts with Dark Sword, Night Armor, and the Codex Umbra.

Codex summons Shadow Puppets (costs 15 HP + 2 Nightmare Fuel + 55 max Sanity each). Puppets chop, mine, or dig automatically for ~2.5 days. Max 3 active, but that reduces usable Sanity to only 35.

75 HP = extremely fragile. Two hits from most bosses kill him. Keep Night Armor equipped always.
›What's Wigfrid's meat-only restriction?
⚔️
Wigfrid
Performance Artist

A method actress stuck in her Viking role. 200 HP, starts with Battle Helm (80% absorption) and Battle Spear (42.5 dmg). Regains HP and Sanity on every kill.

Restriction: only eats Meat and Meat-based Crock Pot dishes. No berries, vegetables, or fruit. Meatballs (1 meat + 3 filler) still work since they're a meat dish.

›How does Webber befriend Spiders?
🕷️
Webber
The Indigestible

Webber is a boy inside the spider that tried to eat him. Counts as a Monster — Spiders are neutral and can be given meat to become followers. He can plant Spider Eggs to create new dens.

Downside: Pigs and Bunnymen attack on sight. 175 HP, 175 Hunger, only 100 Sanity.

Full year = 72 days. 50/50 chance of starting in Autumn or Spring.

›What should I focus on during Autumn?

Days 1–5: Gather 40+ Grass, 40+ Twigs, 10+ Flint. Craft Garland (12 Petals). Explore aggressively — map the coastline.

Days 5–12: Build base. Science Machine → Alchemy Engine. Crock Pot, Fire Pit, Chests, Drying Racks. Start Berry Bush farm.

Days 12–20: Winter prep. Thermal Stone, warm clothing, stockpile food, Lightning Rod.

Bearger can appear in Autumn. Feed him 10 Honey and he'll hibernate. Don't fight unprepared (3000 HP).
›How do I survive Winter?

Temperature drops constantly. At zero you take rapid damage. Essentials:

Thermal Stone — Heats near fire, carries warmth. Non-negotiable.

Winter Hat (Beefalo Wool + Silk) — Good insulation, easy to craft.

Beefalo Hat (Beefalo Horn + 8 Wool) — Best insulation in the game.

Food sources: Rabbit Traps, Koalefants, Crock Pot cooking, Jerky. Walrus Camps activate — farm them for Tam o' Shanter (+6.7 Sanity/min) and Walking Cane (+25% speed).

›When does Deerclops spawn? How to handle it?

Spawns in the last 5 days of Winter (~Day 30). Warning: character says "did you hear that?" followed by louder stomping. 2000 HP, 75 damage, targets structures.

Key: leave your base when you hear stomping! He spawns on top of you. Lead him to Spider Forest or Swamp. To fight: Football Helmet + Log Suit, hit 2–3 times, dodge, repeat.

Drops the Deerclops Eyeball → crafts the Eyebrella — best item in the game (100% rain protection + insulation).

He drains Sanity in a large radius. Bring Jerky or Cooked Green Mushrooms (+15 Sanity) for the fight.
›Why is Spring so annoying?

Constant heavy rain → Wetness → Sanity drain + slipping + soaked items. Bees hostile all season, Beefalo in heat (attack on sight), Rabbit Holes close.

Gear: Eyebrella (best) or Pretty Parasol + Straw Hat. Frog Rains can happen — dozens of Frogs fall from the sky, knock items from inventory.

Moose/Goose spawns near its nest (mossy area). Won't attack unless provoked. Drops Down Feather for the Luxury Fan (Summer cooling).

›How do I keep my base from burning in Summer?

Deadliest season. Overheating = rapid damage. Flammable objects spontaneously combust.

Ice Flingomatic — Auto-extinguishes fires in its radius. Non-negotiable. Place 1–2 to cover your base.

Endothermic Fire Pit — Cools instead of heating.

Summer Frest (3 Blue Gems + 15 Silk) — Best cooling clothing.

Many players spend Summer in the Caves — no overheating or combustion underground.

Dragonfly: 2750 HP, spits lava. Drop 20 Ash nearby to pacify temporarily. Don't engage without full armor.

🍳 Cooking & Crock Pot

The Crock Pot transforms basic ingredients into powerful meals. Master it and you'll never go hungry.

Crock Pot Basics
›How does the Crock Pot work?

Science Machine to prototype. Costs 3 Cut Stone + 6 Charcoal + 6 Twigs. Has 4 slots — fill all 4 to cook (10–40 seconds). Checks ingredients against 58 recipes. No match = Wet Goop.

Finished dishes don't spoil in the pot until you pick them up. Free temporary storage.

›How to avoid Monster Lasagna?

2+ Monster Food items in the pot = Monster Lasagna (−20 HP, −20 Sanity). Never worth eating.

Safe rule: max 1 Monster Meat per recipe. 1 Monster Meat + 3 Berries = Meatballs. Safe and efficient.

›How does food spoilage work?

All food spoils: Fresh → Stale → Spoiled → Rot. Stale = half hunger, −10 Sanity. Spoiled = almost nothing + damage.

Ice Box — Slows spoilage to 50% speed. Essential.

Drying Rack — Turns Meat into Jerky. Jerky lasts 20 days and restores HP + Sanity + Hunger. Build 4+ racks early.

🕷️ Creatures & Combat

Key creatures, their behaviors, stats, drops, and how to fight or avoid them.

Hostile Mobs
›How do Hound attacks work?

Periodic and escalating. First attack ~Day 6–12 with 2–3 Hounds. By Day 100+ you face 7–10 per wave with Fire Hounds (Summer) and Ice Hounds (Winter).

Warnings: character says phrases over 30–60 seconds before they arrive.

Defense: Tooth Trap fields (best), Beefalo herds, Swamp Tentacles, or kiting with Spear + Log Suit. Fire Hounds ignite on death — fight away from flammables.

›How to farm Spider Dens?

Tier 1: 3 Spiders. Tier 2: 6 + Warriors. Tier 3: 9 + Warriors, eventually spawns a Spider Queen.

Drops: Monster Meat, Silk, Spider Glands.

Method: Attack dens during the day. Hit the web to lure 1–2 at a time. For Tier 3, destroy with Hammer to prevent Queen spawn and get Spider Eggs.

Plant Spider Eggs near (not in) your base for a renewable Silk/Gland/Monster Meat farm. 2–3 Tier 1 dens is ideal.
›A Treeguard spawned — what do I do?

Random chance when chopping trees. 2000 HP, hostile, hits hard. But: plant 5–8 Pine Cones near it — it calms down and becomes neutral.

To fight: they're slow, kite easily. Drops 6 Living Logs — needed for Shadow Manipulator and magic items.

Neutral & Passive
›How to interact with Beefalo safely?

Normally neutral. Valuable for: Wool (shave sleeping ones at night), Horn (on death), Manure (they poop constantly).

Spring danger: Mating season (red butts) — hostile to everything. Stay far away.

Build near a herd but not on top of it. Use them as Hound defense by running through during attacks.
›What drops from MacTusk?

Walrus Camps (igloos) active only in Winter. MacTusk spawns every 2.5 days with 2 Blue Hounds. Uses Blow Darts (100 dmg), runs when you close distance.

Drops: Tam o' Shanter (25% — best Sanity hat, +6.7/min), Walking Cane (10% — +25% speed permanently), Blow Dart, Walrus Tusk.

Strategy: Armor up, kill Hounds first, corner MacTusk against terrain.

❤️ Survival Mechanics

Health, Hunger, Sanity — the three pillars. Understanding their interactions is everything.

The Three Stats
›All healing and resurrection methods?

Healing: Spider Gland (+8), Healing Salve (+20), Honey Poultice (+30), Pierogi (+40), Fishsticks (+40), Jerky (+20), Butterfly Wings (+8 raw).

Resurrection:

Touchstones — Pre-placed (usually 2 per map). One-time use. Respawn at 50/50/50. Items left at death location.

Meat Effigy — Crafted (4 Boards + 4 Beard Hair + 4 Meat). Costs 30 max HP while placed.

Life Giving Amulet — Worn. Auto-resurrects with full stats. 3 Gold + 2 Nightmare Fuel + 1 Red Gem.

›What happens at low Sanity?

Screen gets shaky. Below ~80 you see Shadow Creatures (visual only). Below 15% of max, they become physical and attack: Crawling Horror (900 HP, 20 dmg), Terrorbeak (300 HP, 40 dmg).

Drains from: Darkness (−50/min), nearby Monsters (−100 to −400/min), Rain, raw Monster Meat (−15), graves.

Restores from: Flowers (+5), Top Hat (+3.3/min), Tam o' Shanter (+6.7/min), Jerky (+15), Taffy (+15), Tent (+60), prototyping (+15).

Intentionally drop below 15% to farm Shadow Creatures for Nightmare Fuel — required for Magic tier 2. Equip armor, kill them, eat to recover.
›What kills me in total darkness?

Charlie attacks instantly in total darkness — 100 damage per hit with brief pauses. Two hits kills most characters.

Light sources: Campfire/Fire Pit, Torch (75 sec), Miner Hat (8 min, frees hands), Lantern (8 min), Willow's Lighter (infinite, dim).

Miner Hat (1 Straw Hat + 1 Gold + 1 Firefly) is the best portable light. Always carry backup Torch materials.

›Best place to build a base?

Must-haves nearby: Beefalo herd, Rockyland, Grasslands, a Pond.

Nice to have: Pig Village, Swamp (Reeds), Walrus Camp.

Avoid: Swamp (Tentacles wreck structures), Desert (constant Hounds), right on Beefalo (mating = destruction).

Caves have no seasons — no freezing, overheating, or rain. Experienced players use them as a Summer retreat.

🧠 Strategy & Tips

Hard-won wisdom from the Constant. What separates survivors from skeletons.

Survival Strategy
›I keep dying before Day 10. What do I do?

Days 1–3: EXPLORE. Axe + Pickaxe immediately. Gather everything. Garland from 12 Petals. Torches for night travel. Map the coast.

Days 3–6: SETTLE. Science Machine, Fire Pit, Crock Pot, 2 Chests. Backpack. 4 Rabbit Traps near bunny holes.

Days 6–12: ESTABLISH. Alchemy Engine. 20+ Berry Bushes. 4 Drying Racks. Spear + Log Suit. Bee Box. Lightning Rod.

Days 12–20: WINTER PREP. Thermal Stone. Winter Hat. Stockpile food. Locate Walrus Camp. Prestihatitator if possible.

›What is kiting? How on mobile?

Attack → walk away before they hit → walk in → attack again. Most enemies have a wind-up animation.

On mobile/touch: tap red button to attack, joystick to dodge. Timing is tighter, so armor is even more important to absorb mistakes.

On iPhone, the small screen makes kiting harder. If a fight feels risky, don't engage. Most enemies lose interest if you run far enough.
›Most common ways players die?

1. Darkness. No fire or torch. Always carry materials (2 Grass + 2 Twigs).

2. Hound attacks unprepared. No armor, no traps. Wear armor when you hear warnings.

3. First Winter with no prep. No Thermal Stone, no clothing, no food. Prep by Day 12.

4. Deerclops at your base. Leave when you hear stomping. Lead him elsewhere.

5. Picking unwinnable fights. Know enemy stats before engaging.

6. Sanity spiraling. Keep a hat equipped, eat Jerky, pick Flowers.

›What's Adventure Mode?

Find Maxwell's Door (random spawn in Sandbox). Walk through = 5-chapter campaign with unique rules per chapter. You lose all inventory upon entering. Keep 4 items between chapters.

Completing all 5 unlocks Maxwell. Chapter 4 "Darkness" is hardest (almost permanent night). Chapter 5 is a finale with no combat.

Best items to carry forward: Walking Cane, Tam o' Shanter, Dark Sword, Backpack.

›Tips specific to iOS Pocket Edition?

Controls: Red = attack. Green = interact. Orange = cycle action. Optional joystick. Tap objects to target.

Performance: Runs well on iPhone 8+. Save frequently — autosave at dawn but crashes happen.

Includes full RoG content (4 seasons, Wigfrid, Webber, Giants). Apple Arcade version = same content, no ads.

Wall placement is terrible on touchscreen. Use Tooth Traps instead — easier to place and more effective.
›I survived 100 days. What now?

Ruins diving — Caves → Ruins for Ancient tech. Thulecite Crown (90% absorb + sanity aura) and Lazy Explorer (teleport).

Adventure Mode — Beat all 5 chapters, unlock Maxwell.

Kill all Giants — Collect all unique drops.

Mega base — Build everything, organize farms, roads (Cobblestones = speed).

Wes run — Survive 100 days as Wes. The ultimate challenge.
